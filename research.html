Hyperspectral guided image enhancement

The primary contribution of this work lies in incorporating hyperspectral imagery (HSI) into computer vision applications. The proposed approach benefits from the spatial-spectral feature learning and is free from the intermediate computation of transmission map. HSI acquires spectral signatures from different wavelengths. We hypothesize that multi-band information in the reconstructed HSI can improve the perceptual quality of images and thereby generate better results in the respective problem domains. We use Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) for reconstructing 31 maps from the input three channel maps of a RGB image, in an unsupervised fashion which incorporates aids like domain classifier, spectral profile optimization, latent space exploration etc. The generated maps are then processed further using another GAN for removing various image degradations. Based on this hypothesis and technique, we have made remarkable contribution in domains like Image Dehazing and Low Light Image Enhancement, which were recognised at International venues including WACV'21 , AAAI'21, and CVPRW'20.

Aerial image analysis

Deep learning based techniques are utilized for performing scene parsing on aerial images obtained from UAVs. Some challenges that make study in this field interesting are the presence of a large number of objects, cluttered states, and a wide FoV. Our group has proposed methods that perform instance segmentation and object detection with a high degree of accuracy, by integrating improvements such as better spatial context; efficient feature flow, and moderating high scale variations. Aerial image processing in extreme conditions has also been explored by leveraging the strength of self-supervised networks in brightness reconstruction of low light aerial scenes. A new synthetic low-light aerial image dataset has also been proposed, consisting of aerial images with object annotations at the instance level.
